About Konfirmata

Financial identity infrastructure for businesses formal systems overlook

Konfirmata's mission is to turn daily economic activity into verifiable, durable history that underserved entrepreneurs can use when a lender, program, or institution asks for proof.

Mission

Make invisible business activity visible in a form institutions can trust.

Konfirmata is designed for entrepreneurs whose businesses are active, valuable, and persistent, but whose financial histories are still hard for formal systems to evaluate. The product meets people where they are instead of assuming the paperwork already exists.

The aim is practical rather than abstract: help business owners create durable records, help institutions review them more fairly, and reduce the gap between real economic activity and recognized financial identity.

Mission in one line

Konfirmata is not bookkeeping software. It is infrastructure for building financial records that institutions can actually rely on.

02

Inventors

Built from two sides of the same problem.

Muhammad Bamalli observed the problem from inside formal financial infrastructure. Through senior work in banking supervision and later financial analysis in the United States, he saw how institutions evaluate trust, what documentation they rely on, and why capable businesses are often excluded because the record never formed in the first place.

Ahmed Yaradua brought the co-inventor's perspective from the operator side of the gap: how informal markets actually function, what entrepreneurs need from a record system, and what trust must feel like if the product is going to be used in the real world.

Together they shaped Konfirmata as a system that is institutionally legible without losing relevance to the people whose businesses need it most.

Patent

Protected architecture, practical mission.

Konfirmata's core architecture is protected by USPTO Application No. 63/987,858, filed February 2026. The filing covers the methods and systems for creating verifiable, permanent financial records that lenders and institutions can independently review.

The patent position matters because the underlying system is not a user interface idea. It is infrastructure designed to support lender review, regulatory scrutiny, and independent verification over time.
