How Konfirmata works

Each confirmed transaction becomes part of a protected sequence

The goal is simple in plain language: record what happened, review it, confirm it, and keep it. The technical architecture exists to make that flow trustworthy rather than easy to rewrite later.

Plain-language flow

Record, review, confirm, export.

01

Record

A business owner speaks or types a transaction such as “Sold rice for 15,000.” The product matches real work, not formal accounting forms.

02

Review

The app interprets the transaction and reads back a plain-language summary before anything is written permanently.

03

Confirm

This is the decisive step. Without confirmation, there is no permanent entry and nothing is added to the ledger.

04

Export

When needed, the user generates a financial history summary containing the integrity data required for later review.

02

Technical foundation

Append-only records linked by a Cryptographic hash chain.

Confirmation-gated authority

Typing is not enough. The user must explicitly confirm before a ledger record can exist.

Append-only ledger

After confirmation, the entry is not editable, not deletable, and not backdatable. New events become new records.

Cryptographic hash chain

Each record contains a cryptographic link to the previous record, creating a tamper-evident sequence over time.

Previous-entry linkage

If an older record were changed, the chain after it would no longer line up cleanly. That is what makes silent tampering easier to detect.

03

Trust language

What the core trust terms mean in practice.

Confirmation-gated means the user is the authority that forms the permanent record. Nothing becomes final because of an autosave, a background process, or a later admin edit.

Independent verification means the export contains enough integrity data to let another party re-check the record path without needing direct access to the original phone or a lender-side Konfirmata server.

Those two ideas work together: the record is formed intentionally, then preserved in a way that can be reviewed later.

What the export carries

  • The period window covered by the export
  • The count of entries included in that record window
  • Integrity data derived from the included ledger sequence
  • A summary ready for lender, program, or audit review
  • Enough structure to re-check the history without depending on a live app session

The point of the technical model is not to add jargon. It is to make trust legible for both entrepreneurs and institutions when the record finally needs to travel.
