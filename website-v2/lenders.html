For lenders and institutions

Supplemental underwriting input for thin-file borrowers

Konfirmata helps CDFIs, credit unions, community banks, micro-lenders, SBA lenders, and nonprofit programs review borrower activity that is real but weakly documented. It does not replace underwriting. It gives teams a better starting record.

Where it fits

Built for borrowers with operating history and limited paperwork.

Konfirmata sits upstream of the credit decision. Borrowers capture daily operating activity in a structured format; institutions receive a clearer summary of continuity, timing, and exportable history when formal statements are absent or incomplete.

That makes it useful for thin-file applicants, early-stage businesses, informal operators, and community borrowers who have real cash flow but limited conventional documentation. It is especially relevant to programs built to widen access rather than just screen out applicants with incomplete paperwork.

The practical value is simple: less information asymmetry, a cleaner review packet, and a more defensible way to understand borrower-supplied history.

What lenders get

  • Structured transaction histories from borrowers who previously had none
  • Continuity indicators that show consistent recording over time
  • Exportable summaries in standard lender-review formats
  • A documented review trail for AML/CFT and program records
  • Reduced information asymmetry for thin-file borrower evaluation

What the export contains

One structured report your team can review, attach, and discuss.

01

Income statement

Period covered, total inflows by category (sales, other receipts), total outflows (purchases, operating expenses), and net income for the recorded window.

02

Monthly cash flow

A month-by-month table of inflows, outflows, and net — giving reviewers a trend view rather than just a total.

03

Transaction ledger

A full appendix of every confirmed entry — type, label, amount, and timestamp — so a reviewer can trace the borrower's activity directly rather than relying on a summary alone.

04

Tamper-evident seal

A record integrity check and, where available, a public verification link confirming the history has not been altered — reviewable without contacting the borrower or accessing our servers.

The export is a single structured file. It is designed to sit alongside existing underwriting packets as supporting documentation, not to replace them.
